The Effects of Using IT

Topic 5 · Sections 5.1 – 5.2

5.1 Microprocessor-Controlled Devices

A microprocessor-driven labour-saving machine enables users to focus on other activities while the machine completes its work. Second-generation devices are more user-friendly with greater functionality (e.g. "intelligent tuning" in TVs).

Advantages of Labour-Saving Devices

  • No need to do manual tasks at home
  • More time for leisure activities
  • No need to stay home while chores are done
  • Web-enabled devices can be switched on/off remotely
  • Automated burglar alarms give higher sense of security
  • Smart fridges/freezers track expiry dates and prevent food waste

Disadvantages of Labour-Saving Devices

  • Unhealthy lifestyle due to inactivity
  • Tends to make people lazy
  • Potential de-skilling
  • Cybersecurity threats — devices with internet access are vulnerable to hacking

General Effects of Microprocessor-Controlled Devices

General Advantages

  • Save energy — efficient and turn off after inactivity
  • Easier to 'programme' tasks rather than turning knobs and pressing buttons manually

General Disadvantages

  • Can lead to more wasteful devices
  • More complex to operate for technophobes
  • Leaving devices on standby is wasteful

Data Security Issues with Smart Devices

Any home appliance managed remotely could give hackers access to personal information. Devices often have default (or no) passwords — easy for attackers to access. Examples:

  • Hacking a central heating controller can reveal holiday dates → house becomes a burglary target
  • A fridge that automatically orders food can expose credit card numbers to hackers

Social Interactions & IT

Positive Social Aspects

  • Easier to make new friends using chat rooms
  • Easier to find people who share similar interests/hobbies
  • Less expensive to keep in touch using VoIP technology

Negative Social Aspects

  • People do not meet face-to-face as much — social isolation
  • Lack of social interaction may make people more anxious about meeting in real life
  • People can act less politely or more harshly online
  • Cybercrime is a serious issue, especially for teenagers

Monitoring & Controlling Transport

Microprocessors are used extensively in modern transport systems:

Uses

  • Monitoring traffic on motorways
  • Congestion zone monitoring
  • Automatic Number Plate Recognition ANPR
  • Automatic traffic light control
  • Air traffic control systems
  • Railway signalling systems

Advantages:

  • Smart motorways update according to changing conditions
  • More efficient traffic flow
  • ANPR can remotely penalise traffic offences
  • ANPR can spot car thefts
  • Minimises human error — reduces accident rates

Disadvantages:

  • Hacker could gain control of the vehicle
  • System halts entirely if it malfunctions
  • Poor design could compromise safety
  • ANPR tracks innocent people's movements — privacy concern

Autonomous Vehicles

Autonomous cars use sensors, cameras, actuators, microprocessors and complex algorithms to operate.

Example: Autonomous car approaching red traffic light → Control system recognises sign → Checks database for action → CPU instructs actuators to brake and shift to park → Monitors until green → CPU instructs gear engagement, release brakes, open throttle

Advantages of Autonomous Cars/Buses/Vans

  • Safer — removes human error
  • Better for environment — more efficient operation
  • Reduced traffic congestion
  • Increased lane capacity
  • Reduced travel times
  • Stress-free parking for motorists

Disadvantages

  • Very high installation prices
  • Driver and passenger reluctance
  • Reduction in taxi needs → unemployment
  • Sensors and cameras require constant maintenance
  • Ever-present fear of vehicle hacking

Autonomous Trains & Aeroplanes

Autonomous Trains

Advantages:

  • Improved punctuality
  • Reduced running costs — fewer staff
  • No human error
  • Minimised energy consumption
  • Increased frequency of trains — no delay risk
  • Easier to update train schedule changes

Disadvantages:

  • Fear of system being hacked
  • Very high initial installation costs
  • Passenger behaviour monitoring vital especially at rush hours
  • Human reluctance to new technology
  • Without drivers, continuous CCTV monitoring required

Autonomous Aeroplanes

Advantages:

  • Improvement in passenger comfort
  • Reduced running costs — fewer staff
  • Most accidents due to pilot error — safety improved
  • Improved aerodynamics — no cockpit needed

Disadvantages:

  • Security concerns — no pilot on board
  • Emergencies difficult to deal with
  • Vulnerability to hacking
  • Passenger reluctance
  • Software glitches could be catastrophic

5.2 Health Problems from Prolonged IT Use

Long-term exposure to computing devices may affect user well-being:

Health IssueCausePrevention / Minimising Risk
RSI
Repetitive Strain Injury		 Repetitive movements and overuse of muscles such as wrists Regular breaks; ergonomic equipment; correct typing techniques
Back & Neck Strain Prolonged use of monitors; poor posture Adjustable chairs; footrests; tiltable screens
Eyestrain Bad lighting and prolonged screen usage Use LCDs instead of CRTs; take regular breaks; anti-glare screens; regular eye testing
Headaches Incorrect lighting; screen reflections; flickering screens Anti-glare screens; regular breaks; regular eye testing
Ozone Irritation Inhalation of ozone released by laser printers Proper ventilation; house laser printers in a separate room; replace with inkjet printers if possible
