Mobile Communication

Mobile devices support many forms of communication:

SMS Messaging

Quick communication via typed text. Recipient can read it at any time — no need to be available when sent.

Phone Calls

Most fundamental mobile feature. Compact & battery-powered — usable anywhere with a network signal.

VoIP

Voice over Internet Protocol — converts sound into data packets transmitted over the internet.

Video Calls

Real-time video conversations. Requires camera-enabled device and internet. Examples: FaceTime, Skype, Zoom.

Internet Access

Mobile devices connect to the internet via wireless broadband or mobile phone networks (3G/4G/5G).

6.2 Computer Modelling Applications

Applications

  • Personal Finance — simulates investment strategies, retirement planning, risk assessment
  • Bridge & Building Design — simulates structural behaviour, load capacities, response to forces
  • Flood Water Management — predicts flood extents, designs mitigation measures (levees, zoning)
  • Traffic Management — simulates traffic flow, predicts congestion, optimises signal timings
  • Weather Forecasting — simulates atmosphere and oceanic conditions to predict temperature, precipitation, wind, storms

Advantages of Computer Modelling over Humans

  • Less expensive than building the real thing
  • Safer to test dangerous scenarios
  • Try various scenarios in advance
  • More precise and accurate results
  • Analyses large datasets faster
  • Can be reused and updated easily

Disadvantages

  • Output entirely based on input — not 100% reliable
  • Can be very costly
  • May miss nuanced/intangible human factors
  • Based on assumptions — uncertainty exists
  • Struggles with unforeseen or rapidly changing conditions

6.3 Computer-Controlled Systems

Robotics in Manufacturing

Automated machines perform tasks like assembly, welding, packaging — mimic human actions with precision and speed.

Advantages:

  • Work in harmful/hazardous environments
  • Work non-stop
  • Low running costs (fewer employees/wages)
  • Higher productivity and consistency
  • Can do tedious, repetitive tasks
  • Different end-effectors for different tasks

Disadvantages:

  • Difficult to handle unusual tasks
  • Causes higher unemployment
  • De-skilling of workforce
  • Can move factory headquarters internationally
  • High initial setup and maintenance costs

General Computer-Controlled Systems

Also includes: Production line control (monitors, coordinates, optimises manufacturing), Autonomous vehicles (self-driving cars/drones)

General Advantages over Humans:

  • Increased efficiency and productivity
  • Greater consistency
  • Works in any environment
  • Reduced human error
  • Can work with large or delicate items

General Disadvantages:

  • Very high installation and maintenance costs
  • Dependency on reliability
  • Lack of flexibility
  • Requires costly backup systems

6.4 School Management Systems

Comprehensive software automating admin tasks: attendance, grading, scheduling, student info management.

System Advantages:

  • Absenteeism addressed more quickly
  • Parents kept updated
  • Reduced workload for teachers

System Disadvantages:

  • Staff training required
  • High initial software cost
  • Unauthorised access must be prevented (personal data)

Attendance Methods

  • Magnetic ID Cards — students swipe on entry/exit; stores name, school, DOB, registration ID
  • Biometrics — fingerprint stored in database; student scans finger to enter/exit

Biometrics vs Magnetic ID Cards

Biometrics Advantages over ID Cards:

  • Unique fingerprints prevent attendance forgery
  • Cards can be easily cloned
  • Cards can be lost (portable)

Biometrics Disadvantages:

  • Time-consuming to collect all fingerprints initially
  • Expensive fingerprint-reading equipment
  • Cut/damaged finger → access denied
  • Invasion of privacy — fingerprints stored in database

Computer-Aided Learning (CAL)

Technology helps students acquire academic material. Supplements classroom instruction. Students take online assessments to track progress. Cannot always assist with every problem — additional support may be needed.

Applications

6.6 Banking — Automated Teller Machines (ATMs)

ATMs allow customers to perform banking transactions using credit/debit cards.

ATM Transaction Sequence

  1. Customer inserts card → contact made with bank's computer
  2. PIN entered → checked for correctness; card validated; card blocked if stolen or too many failed attempts
  3. Options displayed: change PIN, balance, cash, bill pay, mini-statement
  4. Customer selects cash → account checked for sufficient funds and daily limit
  5. If all checks pass → transaction authorised → cash counted and dispensed
  6. Receipt offered → card returned → account updated

Advantages:

  • Withdraw cash at any time of day
  • Many banking services without visiting a branch
  • Access account from anywhere in the world
  • Usually quicker than queuing in a bank

Disadvantages:

  • Often in places where theft can occur at night
  • Potential for shoulder surfing
  • Some banks charge customers for use
  • Cash withdrawal limits imposed

6.6 Banking — EFT, Credit/Debit Cards & Cheques

Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)

Steps: Initiation → Authorisation → Processing → Transfer of funds → Confirmation

Adv: Very secure; very quick; less expensive than cheques; dispute right up to 60 days

Dis: Transaction can't be reversed without dispute investigation; funds needed immediately; can't guarantee recipient's identity

Cheques

Adv: Safer/more convenient than cash; can be post-dated; traceable if lost

Dis: Not legal tender — can be refused; slow payment; easier for fraudsters; relatively expensive

Credit vs Debit Cards

Credit Card Adv: Customer protection if goods don't arrive; internationally accepted; interest-free loan if paid in time; online purchases

Credit Card Dis: High interest rates; annual fees; risk of credit damage; security risks online

Debit Card Adv: No interest — money from current account; safer than cash; online purchases

Debit Card Dis: Less customer protection; funds needed immediately; security risks online

Internet Banking

Online access to banking services via a secure website or app — transfers, bill payments, account management.

Advantages:

  • Convenience — available 24/7
  • Easy account management
  • No need to visit a branch
  • Interest rates may be better (lower overheads)
  • Easier to shop around for the best account

Disadvantages:

  • Security of transactions is a concern
  • Requires reliable internet connection
  • Higher risk of fraud
  • Easier to make errors
  • Physical cash cannot be deposited or withdrawn

6.7 Computers in Medicine

Patient Records

Characteristics: Accurate and complete; confidential; up-to-date; organised (personal info, history, medications, tests); accessible to authorised staff; legally important

Uses: Correct diagnoses and treatment; tracking patient history; communication between healthcare professionals; legal/insurance evidence; research and planning

Pharmacy Records

Characteristics: Accurate with drug names, doses, quantities; confidential; systematic; traceable; regulated

Uses: Correct dispensing; prevent medication errors and drug interactions; track inventory; auditing compliance; monitor patient medication history

3D Printing in Medicine

  • Prosthetics: artificial limbs, false teeth, hearing aids — less expensive than traditional methods
  • Tissue Engineering: combines cells, biomaterials and engineering to create functional tissues/organs for repair
  • Artificial Blood Vessels: synthetic structures that mimic natural vessels to support tissue repair
  • Customised Medicines: personalised drug formulations with accurate dosages tailored to individual patients

6.8 Expert Systems

AI-based programs that emulate human expert decision-making using knowledge and rules.

Examples: Medical diagnosis · Mineral prospecting · Car engine fault diagnosis · Tax/financial calculations · Strategy games · Plant/animal identification · Route scheduling

5 Components

  • User Interface — the only part the end user sees; allows interaction; often needs training
  • Explanation System — explains reasoning behind conclusions; gives probability percentages
  • Inference Engine — main processing element; acts as search engine on the knowledge base; problem-solving part
  • Knowledge Base — repository of facts; stores all domain knowledge; collection of objects and attributes
  • Rules Base — set of inference rules used by inference engine to draw conclusions; follows logical thinking

How an Expert System Works (Medical Example)

  1. Interactive screen asks doctor questions (keyboard or touch screen)
  2. Inference engine compares symptoms with knowledge base using rule base
  3. System suggests possible illness with probability percentages, cures, and next steps
  4. Explanation system explains how the diagnosis was reached

6.9 Computers in the Retail Industry

POS Terminals

Point of Sale — computerised checkout systems handling purchases and stock.

  • Update stock files immediately after each sale
  • Track inventory levels in real-time
  • Prevent stock discrepancies
  • Auto-reorder when stock falls below predefined value
  • Streamline supply chain management

Internet Shopping

Customer Adv: Global market access; easy comparison; shop from anywhere; avoid queues

Business Adv: Target prices from buying data; quick stock updates; cheaper promotions; international customer base; lower staff costs

Customer Dis: Security concerns; can't examine product; slow delivery; extra shipping costs

Business Dis: Increased competition; fraud; technical issues; delivery costs; fake reviews

EFTPOS Terminals

Electronic funds transfer at point of sale — enables electronic card payments in real-time.

  • Chip & PIN: two-factor authentication; customer enters PIN
  • Contactless: faster; tap card on terminal
  • NFC: payments via smartphones and devices
  • Bank communication: share transaction details securely; instant payment verification
Chip & PIN visual: metal chip on front of card → insert and enter PIN
Contactless visual: wave symbol → tap card
NFC visual: "tap to pay" symbol → wireless payment

Automatic Number Plate Recognition (ANPR)

Step-by-step process:

  1. Sensor triggers camera to capture image of vehicle's front
  2. Algorithm locates and isolates the number plate
  3. Brightness and contrast of plate adjusted
  4. Each character segmented individually
  5. OCR software recognises each character
  6. Characters converted to editable text string
  7. Text string stored in database
  8. Barrier raised; motorist issued a ticket with date/time
  9. On return, ticket inserted → charges calculated → payment registered

Advantages:

  • Automatically monitors average speed over a stretch of road
  • No need for car park attendants or security guards
  • Faster than manual ticket checking at exit
  • Auto-controls entry/exit to car parks or private roads
  • Prevents illegal parking
  • Analyses driver behaviour
  • Inner-city congestion charging systems

Disadvantages:

  • No manned surveillance → risk of vandalism
  • Privacy invasion — records drivers' plates
  • Damaged/dirty plates not recognised
  • Cloned number plates can bypass security

6.11 Satellite Systems

GPS — Global Positioning System

Determines exact location of transport using satellites orbiting Earth. Uses atomic clocks (accurate to a fraction of a second/day). Requires signals from at least 3 satellites to calculate position.

Satellite Navigation (Satnav)

Used to determine exact car location and give directions to a destination.

Adv: No need for paper maps; warns of speed changes; shows ETA; programs fastest route; locates petrol stations

Dis: Outdated maps give incorrect instructions; road closures cause problems; loss of satellite signal; incorrect input → wrong directions

GIS — Geographic Information Systems

Captures, manages, analyses and displays geographically referenced information. Used to: determine distance between places; find nearest petrol station/restaurant; protect wildlife; plan infrastructure.

Media Communication Satellites

Satellite Television: signals broadcast from satellites to dishes; wide coverage; independent of ground cables

Satellite Phone: communication via satellite rather than ground towers; works in remote areas without mobile coverage
