Topic 9 · Sections 9.1 – 9.2

9.1 Audience Appreciation

Expressing gratitude and value for users by providing helpful and relevant information, fostering positive experiences, and contributing to their understanding and learning.

Planning ICT Solutions

  • First identify and understand the intended audience's needs — functional (processing info) or aesthetic (visually appealing interface)
  • Select appropriate technology and tools based on task complexity, budget, and users' technical expertise
  • Ensure solutions are user-friendly — intuitive, require minimal training, easy-to-understand instructions
  • Solutions must be accessible to all users, including those with disabilities — voice commands, larger text, compatibility with assistive devices
  • Test the solution to ensure it functions as expected and fulfils users' needs

Analysing Audience Requirements

  • Consider factors affecting the audience's ability to use the solution: age, technical proficiency, expertise
  • Understand the audience's information requirements — influences data processing and information architecture design
  • Consider how the audience consumes content — e.g. if mostly mobile, solution must be mobile-friendly
  • Consider any unique requirements — visual or auditory impairments must be accommodated to ensure inclusivity

9.2 Copyright

Why is Copyright Necessary?

  • Safeguards the rights of developers and innovators
  • Stops software piracy — unauthorised use, replication or distribution
  • Guarantees developers receive compensation for their efforts, fostering further innovation

Principles of Copyright in Computer Software

  • Copyright law prohibits unauthorised copying, distribution or modification of software
  • Includes the EULA (End-User Licence Agreement) — outlines what the software can and cannot be used for
  • Violations (such as software piracy) can lead to legal consequences

Preventing Software Copyright Violation

  • DRM (Digital Rights Management) — methods and systems that restrict usage, modification and distribution of copyrighted works
  • Software Product Activation — requires a unique activation code for the software to be fully functional
  • Automatic Updates — pirated software cannot receive automatic upgrades, making it less effective and secure over time; this itself acts as a copyright protection mechanism
Software Piracy = unauthorised copying, sharing or use of software without a valid licence.
